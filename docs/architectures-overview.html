Synthesis · What actually recurs

The moves that show up again and again

Strip away the framework wars and the leaderboard converges on a handful of decisions. These are the ones worth copying.

Backbone 7 / 10

1D residual net with SE attention

Squeeze-excitation on a 1D ResNet is the default winning core. It is the safest single bet in the whole field.

Front-end 10 / 10

Resample to 500 Hz, fixed window

Everyone standardizes sampling rate, crops/pads to a fixed length (10–15 s), and normalizes gain before the net sees anything.

Side info 5 / 10

Fuse age, sex & hand-crafted feats

Demographics and HRV/statistical features are concatenated to deep features right before the classifier — cheap, consistent lift.

Objective 10 / 10

Weighted multi-label BCE + thresholds

Sigmoid outputs, class-frequency-weighted BCE, and per-class decision thresholds tuned on validation to the challenge metric.

Robustness 3 / 10

Variable length via adaptive pooling

Adaptive max/avg pooling (or flagged concatenation) lets one model ingest recordings of any duration without retraining.

Ensembling 4 / 10

Stratified folds, blended models

Stratified multi-label splits plus averaging several backbones/seeds is how the top ranks squeeze out the last points.

Loss & balance

  • Weighted BCE with 1/log(freq) class weights
  • Optional focal term for rare classes
  • Class-balanced or stratified multi-label sampling

Optimization

  • Adam, LR 1e-3 → staged decay
  • Batch 64 · 30–40 epochs
  • Zero-init residual BN for stable start

Generalization

  • 5-fold stratified ensemble + seed averaging
  • Optional domain-adversarial branch (Team 05)
  • Optional self-supervised pretrain (Team 07)

Inference

  • Sliding-window over long records, average logits
  • Apply tuned per-class thresholds
  • Blend fold models for the final vote
